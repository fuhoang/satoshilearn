---
title: Exchange Risks
---

Exchanges are useful for buying and selling bitcoin, but they also introduce third-party risk. When bitcoin sits on an exchange, the platform controls the keys. That means your access depends on the exchange remaining solvent, secure, compliant, and willing to process withdrawals.

This setup can be convenient, especially for beginners, but it changes the nature of ownership. You may see a balance on screen, yet you are still trusting someone else to honor that balance and give you access when requested.

History has shown that exchanges can fail in many ways. They can be hacked, mismanaged, frozen by regulation, or simply collapse under poor internal controls. In each case, users discover that convenience is not the same as possession.

This does not mean exchanges are useless. They are often the easiest place to enter the market. But they are better thought of as service providers than long-term vaults. The more important self-custody becomes to you, the more seriously you should think about moving funds off platforms when appropriate.

The main takeaway is simple: exchanges are tools, not neutral storage. Understand the risk before treating them like a permanent home for your bitcoin.
