---
title: Phishing and Fake Apps
---

Phishing is when an attacker tricks you into giving up sensitive information by pretending to be a trusted service. In Bitcoin, phishing often targets passwords, wallet access, seed phrases, or exchange accounts. Fake apps do the same thing through software that looks legitimate but is designed to steal.

These attacks work because they imitate what users already expect. A fake wallet app may copy the branding of a real wallet. A phishing email may look like a normal security alert. A scam website may copy the design of an exchange login page. The goal is to make you act automatically instead of carefully.

The best defense is slowing down. Check URLs. Download apps only from trusted sources. Be suspicious of urgent messages telling you to recover, verify, or unlock something immediately. And never enter a seed phrase into a random site just because a message claims it is required.

Attackers often rely on emotional pressure. They want fear, confusion, or excitement to override basic verification habits. That is why calm process matters so much in Bitcoin security.

A practical beginner rule is this: if a message creates urgency and asks for sensitive information, assume it may be malicious until proven otherwise.
