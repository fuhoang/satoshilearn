---
title: Energy and Bitcoin
---

Bitcoin uses energy because Proof of Work requires miners to perform real computation. That energy cost is not accidental. It is part of how the network makes attacks expensive and block production hard to fake.

This is why debates about Bitcoin and energy can become confused. Some people treat energy use as automatically wasteful. Others treat all energy use as justified because it secures the network. A better approach is to ask what the system is buying with that energy and whether the tradeoff is worthwhile.

Bitcoin is buying security, openness, and resistance to central control. The network does not get those properties for free. The energy cost is tied to the fact that miners must commit real resources rather than just claim authority.

At the same time, it is reasonable to ask where that energy comes from, how efficiently it is used, and what tradeoffs exist compared with other systems. Energy discussions should be concrete, not emotional. Different mining operations use different energy sources, and the topic is more nuanced than simple slogans suggest.

The beginner takeaway is this: Bitcoin uses energy because its security model depends on real-world cost. The real question is not whether it uses energy, but what that energy secures and whether that is valuable.
