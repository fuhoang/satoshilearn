---
title: Difficulty and Hash Rate
---

Hash rate is a rough measure of how much computational power miners are applying to Bitcoin. The more hash rate on the network, the more work is being performed in the race to find the next block. It is often treated as a signal of how much security and investment the network currently has behind it.

Difficulty is the adjustment mechanism Bitcoin uses to keep block production roughly on schedule. If miners collectively find blocks too quickly, the network raises difficulty. If blocks are being found too slowly, the network lowers it. This keeps Bitcoin from speeding up or slowing down too much when mining power changes.

These two ideas are closely related. More hash rate usually means blocks would be found faster, but the difficulty adjustment counters that by making the puzzle harder. Less hash rate usually means the opposite, and difficulty eventually adjusts downward.

For beginners, the point is not to memorize formulas. The point is to understand that Bitcoin responds automatically to changing mining conditions. It does not need a central operator to manually rebalance the system.

Difficulty and hash rate show how Bitcoin turns a competitive global process into a relatively stable block schedule.
