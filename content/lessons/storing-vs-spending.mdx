---
title: Storing vs Spending
---

One of the most common questions beginners ask is whether Bitcoin is mainly for saving or for spending. The honest answer is that it can be both, but people often prioritize one use over the other depending on their goals.

If someone treats Bitcoin as long-term savings, they may focus on self-custody, security, and a longer time horizon. In that mindset, spending is not the main point. Preservation and conviction matter more. If someone treats Bitcoin more like a transaction tool, ease of movement and payment convenience may matter more.

These are not mutually exclusive uses. A person can keep most of their bitcoin in long-term storage while still using a smaller amount for active spending or experimentation. Different tools and wallet setups may suit those different purposes.

The mistake is assuming there must be only one correct use. Bitcoin is a monetary tool with multiple possible roles. The right balance depends on your needs, risk tolerance, and understanding.

A helpful beginner mindset is this: first learn to store safely, then decide deliberately how much you want to keep versus spend.
