---
title: Who Created Bitcoin?
---

Bitcoin was introduced by someone using the name Satoshi Nakamoto. In October 2008, Satoshi published the Bitcoin white paper. In January 2009, the first version of the software was released and the network began operating. That launch included both an idea and a working implementation, which is one reason Bitcoin was taken seriously from the beginning.

No one knows with certainty who Satoshi really is. The name could belong to one person or a group. Over the years, many people have claimed to know, guessed identities, or tried to prove authorship, but no public claim has been accepted as definitive. For practical purposes, Satoshi remains pseudonymous.

That mystery attracts attention, but it is not the most important part of the story. What matters more is that Bitcoin was designed so it would not need its creator forever. Satoshi wrote code, communicated with early contributors, and helped the network get off the ground, but the project was open-source from the start. Anyone could inspect it, copy it, improve it, or disagree with it.

By 2010 and 2011, Satoshi had largely stepped away. Development and discussion continued without a founder acting as a permanent ruler. This is unusual in technology. Many systems remain deeply dependent on the company, founder, or foundation behind them. Bitcoin's credibility is stronger precisely because it kept going after its creator disappeared.

This is part of why people often say Bitcoin has no CEO and no central owner. There are influential developers, miners, businesses, and educators, but there is no final authority who can simply rewrite the rules for everyone else. That makes Bitcoin harder to control, but also harder to capture.

So who created Bitcoin? Satoshi Nakamoto did. Who is Satoshi Nakamoto? We do not know for sure. But the deeper lesson is that Bitcoin was built to be bigger than its founder. That is one reason it is treated less like a startup and more like a protocol.
