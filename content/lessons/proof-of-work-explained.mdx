---
title: Proof of Work Explained
---

Proof of Work is the mechanism Bitcoin uses to make block production costly and verifiable. Miners must perform repeated computations to find a block hash that meets the network's current difficulty target. This is easy for the network to check, but expensive to produce at scale.

Why does that matter? Because cost creates resistance. If an attacker wants to rewrite Bitcoin's recent history, they would need to reproduce enormous amounts of work and outrun the honest network. That makes attacks expensive instead of trivial.

Proof of Work also helps the network choose one accepted chain without relying on trust in one operator. The chain with the most accumulated work becomes the reference point participants coordinate around. In practice, this gives Bitcoin a way to maintain consensus in an open environment.

Critics sometimes focus on the visible energy cost, but that cost is part of the design. Bitcoin uses work precisely because work is hard to fake. The network is buying security by making block production costly.

The simplest takeaway is this: Proof of Work is how Bitcoin turns real-world cost into digital trust minimization.
