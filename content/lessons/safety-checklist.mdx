---
title: Safety Checklist
---

Bitcoin security becomes easier when you reduce it to a checklist. Complex systems feel manageable when you turn them into repeatable habits. The goal is not perfection. The goal is avoiding the obvious mistakes that cause most losses.

Start with the basics. Use trusted wallet software. Back up your seed phrase carefully. Keep it offline and private. Do not share it. Do not type it into random websites. Keep your devices updated and avoid downloading suspicious software.

Next, think about operational habits. Double-check transaction details. Be wary of support messages, giveaways, and urgent recovery requests. If you are moving a meaningful amount, consider sending a small test transaction first.

Also decide how much responsibility you are taking on. If you use self-custody, act like your own security team. If you rely on an exchange, be honest about the tradeoff and do not confuse convenience with control.

A good checklist does not remove all risk, but it lowers the chance of preventable failure. In Bitcoin, that is already a major advantage.
