---
title: Why Miners Exist
---

Miners exist because Bitcoin needs a way to package transactions into blocks and defend the ledger against manipulation. They are rewarded for doing this work honestly, which aligns their incentives with the network's need for ongoing security.

In most systems, a central operator can decide which transactions are final. Bitcoin does not have that central operator. Miners help fill that role in a decentralized way by competing to add valid blocks under public rules.

Their incentive comes from two sources: the block subsidy and transaction fees. The subsidy introduces new bitcoin according to the protocol schedule. Fees come from users who want their transactions included. Together, these rewards give miners a reason to commit resources to the network.

Miners are not rulers of Bitcoin. They cannot simply invent any rule they want. Nodes still verify whether blocks follow the protocol. But miners do play a critical role in turning pending transactions into confirmed history.

So miners exist because Bitcoin needs both economic motivation and real-world cost behind its block production. They are part of the system's security model, not a side feature.
