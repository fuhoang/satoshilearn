---
title: Confirmations Explained
---

A confirmation means a transaction has been included in a block that has been accepted by the Bitcoin network. Once that happens, the transaction is no longer just waiting in the mempool. It is now part of the blockchain.

Each new block added after that increases the confirmation count. One confirmation means the transaction is in the chain. Two confirmations mean one additional block has been built on top of it. Six confirmations means the network would have to rewrite a longer stretch of history to reverse it.

Why does this matter? Because Bitcoin settlement is probabilistic, not magical. A fresh transaction can be displaced or replaced before it is deeply embedded in the chain. The more confirmations it has, the more confidence users have that it is final in practice.

Different situations call for different confirmation expectations. A small in-person payment may be accepted quickly depending on the merchant's risk tolerance. A large transfer usually deserves more caution. There is no universal number that makes sense for every case.

The main lesson is simple: confirmations are how Bitcoin builds confidence over time. They are a practical measure of how settled a transaction is.
