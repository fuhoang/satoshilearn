---
title: Custodial vs Non-Custodial Wallets
---

The biggest wallet decision for a beginner is whether to use custodial or non-custodial storage. This is really a question about who controls the keys. In a custodial setup, another company holds the keys for you. In a non-custodial setup, you hold them yourself.

Custodial wallets are common on exchanges and apps that want to make onboarding simple. They often feel familiar because they work like online banking. If you forget a password, there may be recovery support. The tradeoff is that you do not have full control. You are trusting the provider to remain solvent, honest, secure, and available.

Non-custodial wallets give you direct control. That means no company can freeze access to your funds or spend them on your behalf. It also means you are responsible for backups, device security, and recovery. With freedom comes operational responsibility.

Neither option is automatically right for every person or every stage. A beginner with a very small amount may temporarily use a custodial service while learning. But to understand Bitcoin properly, it is important to understand why self-custody is different and why many people consider it one of Bitcoin's most important features.

The clearest way to compare them is this: custodial wallets optimize convenience, while non-custodial wallets optimize control. The more value you are protecting, the more that distinction matters.
