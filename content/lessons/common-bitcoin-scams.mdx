---
title: Common Bitcoin Scams
---

Most Bitcoin scams are not technically sophisticated. They usually rely on urgency, greed, confusion, or fake authority. The scammer's goal is to get you to send funds, reveal recovery information, or trust the wrong person before you slow down and think clearly.

Common patterns include giveaway scams, fake investment schemes, impersonation of support staff, fake recovery services, and phishing pages that pretend to be wallet or exchange logins. Some scams promise guaranteed returns. Others create panic and tell you your wallet is at risk unless you act immediately.

A useful rule is that Bitcoin transactions are hard to reverse, so scammers love them. Once funds are sent to a bad actor, recovery is usually unlikely. That is why prevention matters far more than trying to fix things afterward.

The safest mindset is skepticism. If something promises easy profit, urgent rescue, or secret access, treat it as suspicious. Legitimate services do not need your seed phrase, and honest opportunities do not depend on you acting before you can verify the details.

Recognizing scams early is one of the most practical Bitcoin skills a beginner can develop.
