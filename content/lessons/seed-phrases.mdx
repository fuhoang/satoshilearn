---
title: Seed Phrases
---

A seed phrase is a human-readable backup for your wallet, usually made of 12 or 24 words. Those words can recreate the wallet's keys if your phone, laptop, or hardware wallet is lost, stolen, or damaged. In practice, the seed phrase is often the most important backup a beginner will ever handle.

If someone gets your seed phrase, they can usually rebuild your wallet and take your bitcoin. If you lose the phrase and your device stops working, you may lose access yourself. That is why seed phrase handling is one of the most critical parts of Bitcoin self-custody.

Good practice starts with keeping the phrase offline, accurate, and private. Write it down carefully. Store it somewhere secure. Double-check that you copied every word in the correct order. Do not treat it like an ordinary password and do not leave it in screenshots, cloud drives, or random notes apps.

Scams often target this exact weak point. Attackers may pretend to be support staff, wallet recovery services, or urgent security alerts. A real service should never need your seed phrase. The moment someone asks for it, you should treat that as a serious red flag.

Seed phrases feel simple because they are just words, but that simplicity hides their power. They are not a reminder. They are a recovery key to the entire wallet. Treat them accordingly.
