---
title: Common Transaction Mistakes
---

Most transaction mistakes are not highly technical. They are ordinary human errors made in a system where reversals are difficult or impossible. That is why careful process matters so much in Bitcoin.

One common mistake is sending to the wrong address. Another is rushing through the process without checking the amount or fee. Beginners also sometimes misunderstand confirmations and assume a payment is fully settled the moment it appears in a wallet.

Another frequent mistake is using the wrong network or service context. If you do not understand where you are sending funds, what wallet type is involved, or what the receiving party expects, you can create confusion or loss. This is especially true when people mix exchange workflows with self-custody workflows without understanding the difference.

People also make mistakes by ignoring small test transactions. For meaningful amounts, it is often worth proving the route first with a small send. That extra step can prevent much larger errors later.

The best protection against transaction mistakes is not brilliance. It is discipline. Slow down, verify details, understand the destination, and treat every meaningful transfer with care.
