---
title: What Are Fees?
---

Bitcoin fees are payments users attach to transactions so miners have an incentive to include them in a block. Fees are not arbitrary charges from a company. They are part of how demand for limited block space gets sorted.

Each block can include only so much transaction data. When many people are trying to send bitcoin at the same time, users effectively compete for inclusion. Higher-fee transactions are more attractive to miners because miners earn those fees when they include the transaction in a valid block.

This means fees are dynamic. They tend to rise when the network is busy and fall when there is less demand. Fees are not mainly about the amount of bitcoin you send. They are more closely related to the size and structure of the transaction data.

For beginners, the key idea is that fees are the price of getting a transaction processed on a decentralized network with limited block space. If you want faster inclusion, you may need to pay more. If you are not in a hurry, a lower fee may still work when the network is less congested.

Fees matter because they are part of Bitcoin's incentive system. They help allocate scarce block space and support network security over time.
