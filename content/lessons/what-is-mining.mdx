---
title: What Is Mining?
---

Mining is the process by which new Bitcoin blocks are added to the blockchain. Miners gather valid transactions, package them into candidate blocks, and compete to solve a computational puzzle. The first one to find a valid solution earns the right to add the block and collect the block reward plus fees.

This does not mean miners are "creating bitcoin out of nowhere" in an arbitrary way. New bitcoin are issued according to the protocol schedule as part of the reward for securing the network. Mining is therefore both an issuance mechanism and a security mechanism.

Miners do real work in the economic sense: they spend energy, buy equipment, and compete under open rules. That cost is part of what makes it expensive to attack the network. If adding blocks were free, it would be much easier to spam or manipulate Bitcoin's history.

For a beginner, the important idea is that mining helps Bitcoin decide which valid block becomes the next block in the chain. It is how the network keeps moving forward without needing a central authority to choose the official ledger.

So mining is not just about "making coins." It is about ordering transactions, securing the chain, and enforcing the system's monetary schedule.
