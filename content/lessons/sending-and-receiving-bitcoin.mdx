---
title: Sending and Receiving Bitcoin
---

Receiving bitcoin starts with generating an address from your wallet. An address is a destination other people can use to send funds to you. Good wallets usually make this simple by presenting a QR code or a copyable text string.

Sending bitcoin means creating a transaction that references your available balance, specifies the destination address, and includes a fee. Before confirming the send, you should always review the address, the amount, and the fee level. Bitcoin transactions are generally not reversible.

For beginners, the safest habit is to slow down. Double-check addresses. If possible, use QR codes or copy-paste rather than typing manually. For larger transfers, many people prefer sending a small test amount first before moving the full balance.

It is also important to understand that receiving is not the same as final settlement. A wallet may show an incoming transaction quickly, but the confidence level increases as confirmations build. The same goes for transactions you send.

The practical rule is this: sending and receiving bitcoin is straightforward once you understand the flow, but accuracy matters. Bitcoin rewards careful habits.
